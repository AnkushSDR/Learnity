Polynomials

1. Introduction to Polynomials

What is a constant?

A symbol having a fixed numerical value is called a constant. Examples: 9, -6, 4/7, √2, π are all constants.

What is a variable?

A symbol which may be assigned different numerical values is known as a variable.
For example, We know that the circumference of a circle is given by the formula C = 2πr, where r is the radius of the circle. Here, 2 and π are constants, while C and r are variables.

What is an algebraic expression?

A combination of constants and variables, connected by some or all of the operations such as +, -, x and ÷, is known as an algebraic expression.

Terms of an Algebraic Expression

The several parts of an algebraic expression separated by + or - operations are called the terms of the expression.

  1. 5x² - 9x + 4 is an algebraic expression containing three terms, namely 5x², -9x and 4.
  2. 4x³ + √2x² - 5/9x - 8 is an algebraic expression containing four terms, namely 4x³, √2x², -5/9x and -8.
  3. y⁶ - 64 is an expression containing two terms, namely y⁶ and -64.

What is the standard property of polynomial?

A polynomial in x expressed either in ascending powers of x or in descending powers of x is said to be in standard form.

2. Polynomial of Various Degrees

Degrees of Polynomial

Linear Polynomial

A polynomial of degree 1 is called alinear polynomial. A linear polynomial in x is of the form, f(x) = ax + b, where a and b are real numbers and a is not equal to 0.
Examples:
(i) 5x + 7 is a linear polynomial in x.
(ii) -8z is a linear polynomial in z.

Quadratic Polynomial

A polynomial of degree 2 is called a quadratic polynomial. Aa quadratic polynomial in x is of the form f(x) = ax² + bx + c, where a, b, and c are real numbers and a is not equal to 0.
Example:
(i) 3x² - 8x + 9 is a quadratic polynomial in x.

Cubic Polynomial

A polynomial of degree 3 is called a cubic polynomial. A cubic polynomial in x is of the form of ax³ + bx² + cx + d, where a, b, c, and d are real numbers and a is not equal to 0.
Example:
(i) 4x³ + x² + 6x + 3 is a cubic polynomial in x

Biquadratic Polynomial

A polynomial of degree 4 is called a biquadratic or quartic polynomial. A biquadratic polynomial in x is of the form of ax^4 + bx³ + cx² + dx + e, where a, b, c, d and e are real numbers and a is not equal to 0.

Number of Terms in a Polynomial

Monomial

A polynomial containing one term is called a monomial.
Example: 9, -14, -6x, -2x and 8y² etc

Binonomial

A polynomial containing two terms is called a binomial.
Example: (9 + 4x), (-14x + 2y), (-6x + 7y), (-2x - 8y²) etc

Trinomial

A polynomial containing three terms is called a trinomial.
Example: (9 + 14x + 7y), (-6x + 2x - 3z), (8y² + 5z + 4x) etc

Other Types of Polynomial

Constant Polynomial

A polynomial containing one term, consisting of a nonzero constant, in called a constant polynomial. In general, every nonzero real number is a constant polynomial. The degree of a nonzero constant polynomial is zero.
Examples: 6, -8, 9/14, -7/12 etc. are all constant polynomials.

Zero Polynomial

Let p(x) be a polynomial. If p(a) = 0 then we say that "a" is a zero of the polynomial p(x).

REMARK: Finding the zeros of a polynomial p(x) means solving the equation p(x) = 0.

Examples Regarding The Topic

Example 1:
If p(x) = x³ + 2x² - 5x - 6, find p(2), p(-1), p(-3) and p(0). What do you conclude about the zeros of p(x)? Is 0 a zero of p(x)?

Solution:
Given, p(x) = x³ + 2x² - 5x - 6.
p(2) = 2³ + 2 x 2² - 5 x 2 - 6 = (8 + 8 - 10 - 6) = 0.
p(-1) = (-1)³ + 2 x (-1)² - 5 x (-1) - 6
= (-1 + 2 + 5 - 6) = 0.
p(-3) = (-3)³ + 2 x (-3)² - 5 x (-3) - 6
= (-27 + 18 + 15 - 6) = 0.
p(0) = 0³ + 2 x 0² - 5 x 0 - 6
= (0 + 0 - 0 - 6) = -6 ≠ 0.
Since p(2) = 0, p(-1) = 0 and p(-3) = 0, it follows that 2, -1 and -3 are the zeros of p(x). But, p(0) = -6 ≠ 0.
Therefore, 0 is not a zero of p(x).

Example 2:
Check whether -2 and 2 are the zeros of the polynomial x + 2.

Solution:
Let p(x) = x + 2. Then,
p(-2) = (-2 + 2) = 0 and p(2) = (2 + 2) = 4 ≠ 0.
Therefore, -2 is a zero of x + 2 and 2 is not a zero of x + 2.

Example 3:
Find a zero of the polynomial: (i) p(x) = x - 3 (ii) q(x) = 3x + 2

Solution:
(i) p(x) = 0 ⇒ x - 3 = 0 ⇒ x = 3.
Therefore, 3 is a zero of the polynomial p(x) = x - 3.
(ii) q(x) = 0
⇒ 3x + 2 = 0
⇒ 3x = -2
⇒ x = -2/3.
Therefore, -2/3 is a zero of the polynomial q(x) = 3x + 2.

4. Division Algorithm

What is the Division Algorithm?

Let p(x) and g(x) be two given polynomials such that degree p(x) ≥ degree g(x). On dividing p(x) by g(x), let q(x) be the quotient and r(x) be the remainder. Then, in general, we have
dividend = (divisor x quotient) + remainder, i.e., p(x) = g(x) ⋅ q(x) + r(x) where r(x) = 0 or degree r(x) is less than degree g(x).

5. Remainder Theorem

What is the Remainder Theorem?

Let p(x) be a polynomial of degree 1 or more and let a be any real number. If p(x) is divided by (x - a) then the remainder is p(a).

PROOF: Let p(x) be a polynomial of degree 1 or more. Suppose that when p(x) is divided by (x-a) then the quotient is q(x) and remainder is r(x).
By division algorithm, we have, p(x) = (x-a)q(x) + r(x), where degree r(x) less than degree (x-a) = 1 [Since, degree (x - a) = 1].

But, degree r(x) less than 1 ⇒ degree r(x) = 0 ⇒ r(x) is a constant, equal to r (say). p(x) = (x-a) q(x) + r. Putting x = a on both sides of (i), we get p(a) = 0xq(x)+r ⇒ r=p(a). Thus, when p(x) is divided by (x - a), then the remainder is p(a).

Examples and Solutions

Example 1:
Find the remainder when the polynomial p(x) = x⁴ + 2x³-3x²+x-1 is divided by g(x) = x - 2

Solution:
g(x)=0 ⇒ x-2 = 0 ⇒ x = 2.
By the remainder theorem, we know that when p(x) is divided by (x-2) then the remainder is p(2).
Now, p(2) = (2⁴ + 2 x 2³ - 3 x 2² + 2 - 1)
= (16+16-12+2-1) = 21.
Hence, the required remainder is 21.

Example 2:
By the remainder theorem, we know that when p(x) is divided by (x + 3) then the remainder is p(-3).

Solution:
By the remainder theorem, we know that when p(x) is divided by (2x - 1) then the remainder is p(1/2).
Now, p(1/2) = [4(1/2)³ - 12(1/2)² + 14 x (1/2) - 3]
= (1/2 - 3 + 7 - 3) = 3/2.
Hence, the required remainder is 3/2.

Example 3:
Find the remainder when the polynomial p(x) = 4x³ - 12x² + 14x - 3 is divided by g(x) = (2x - 1).

Solution:
g(x) = 0 ⇒ 2x - 1 = 0 ⇒ x = 1/2.
By the remainder theorem, we know that when p(x) is divided by (2x - 1) then the remainder is p(1/2).
Now, p(1/2) = [4(1/2)³ - 12(1/2)² + 14 x (1/2) - 3]
= (1/2 - 3 + 7 - 3) = 3/2.
Hence, the required remainder is 3/2.
